Interview with KWC (Creator of Memory Card Game)

ME:

Ok. Firstly, When did you start programming?

How did you learn programming?

What is your best advice to a beginner?

Are you a good card player in real life?

and What is your next project (you dont have to answer this) and when will it be released?

Thanks :D

KWC: Sure. I will answer a few. :-)

1: I started programming in early 2015 but had tried to teach myself Python through trial and error the previous year.

2: Though reading endless forums and documentation and through many hours of experimenting.

3: Don't get overwhelmed. Programming is not easy but can simplified if broken into small parts. Now being bilingual in scripting languages I can say that if you learn one language you can easily learn another. Starting out I think learning JS as your first language is the best way into the somewhat intimidating world of coding. It's much more forgiving than other languages like C++ or Python. Also that even you may have no interest in learning to build webpages, learning HTML is very important to learn about patterns that also apply to scripting.

4: With card games from the SET company I'm pretty good but maybe more average with regular card games.

5: I don't think I will have a next project for awhile. I mainly returned to KA to release Memory.
